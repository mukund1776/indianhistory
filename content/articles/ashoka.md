Ashoka ruled much of the Indian subcontinent after a violent succession and the Kalinga War, which led him to renounce further conquest and promote non-violence.

His rock and pillar edicts record policies on tolerance, welfare, and moral conduct. Ashoka sent missionaries beyond India and helped Buddhism become a major world religion. The relief above, from Sanchi, is among the visual traditions linking Ashoka to Buddhist pilgrimage and the spread of the dharma.
