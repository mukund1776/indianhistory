The Indus Valley Civilization (also called the Harappan Civilization) developed sophisticated cities such as Harappa and Mohenjo-daro, with planned streets, drainage systems, and standardized weights.

Archaeological evidence suggests extensive trade networks and a script that remains undeciphered. The civilization declined around 1900 BCE, possibly due to climate change and shifts in river courses.
